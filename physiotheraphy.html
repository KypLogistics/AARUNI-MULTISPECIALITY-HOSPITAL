Department of Physiotherapy & Rehabilitation

The Department of Physiotherapy aims at restoring, maintaining, and enhancing the patient’s mobility, functioning, and overall well-being. Physiotherapy helps patients by providing complete assistance through physical rehabilitation, managing health and fitness through injury prevention. We recommend patients undergo physiotherapy if they are suffering from chronic pain conditions that are affecting the everyday routine functioning. Physiotherapy can save patients from long-term pain conditions and can enable them to live a pain-free life.

Our physiotherapists emphasize both prevention, rehabilitation, and treatment of major injuries, diseases, and disabilities.

  • Loss of mobility due to spinal trauma or brain injury and conditions like Parkinson’s disease and multiple sclerosis and paralysis
  • Lung problems like Asthma
  • Loss of muscle strength due to fatigue, stiffness, pain, swelling during palliative care for cancer
  • Disability due to heart problems
  • Problems due to pelvic issues like problems in the bladder and bowel and those related to childbirth
  • Pain problems in the bones, muscles, joints, and ligaments due to arthritis and other diseases
The various conditions that can be treated through physiotherapy are as follows:

  • Sports injuries: Whiplash, shin splints, Golfers Elbow, Rotator cuff injuries, tennis elbow, etc.
  • Post-surgery recovery: Shoulder, Hip, Knee, ligament, athletic injury, spinal cord injury,
  • Neurological conditions: brain stroke, traumatic brain injury, Parkinson’s disease, chronic regional pain syndrome, spinal cord injury, cerebral palsy
  • Posture related problems: Neck pain, Upper/ Lower back pain, poor muscle tone, frozen shoulder, muscle imbalance, muscle weakness
  • Musculoskeletal disorders: Osteoarthritis, Carpal Tunnel Syndrome, joint instability, degenerative diseases, age-related joint disorders, bursitis
